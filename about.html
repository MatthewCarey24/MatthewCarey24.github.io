Hi, I'm Matthew.

I'm an engineer currently applying to medical school with a passion for applying computational technology to systems medicine. I'm particularly interested in how computational models and analytical approaches can help us better understand complex biological systems and improve patient outcomes.

I currently split my time between Boston and Philadelphia. In Philly, I work as an Emergency Room scribe. In Boston, I work in a Mechanical Engineering lab at Tufts, conduct clinical follow-up research with an orthopaedic group, and collaborate on research that uses machine learning to develop optimal similarity scoring metrics for mass spectrometry.

Outside of my academic and professional pursuits, I'm passionate about staying active through lifting, running, and martial arts. I love spending time outdoors, obsessing over the perfect coffee brew, experimenting in the kitchen, and playing guitar.
